Painless Root Canal Treatment with Immediate Restoration

A patient presented with severe facial swelling extending to the eye and acute discomfort; a painless RCT with a final cap restored function and aesthetics.

Treatment Summary

The patient presented with an acute endodontic infection causing facial swelling that tracked up to the periorbital area. A single-visit, painless root canal treatment was performed under local anesthesia with attention to infection control, followed by placement of a full-coverage cap to restore strength and appearance.

Clinical Notes

  • Acute facial swelling extending to the eye — urgent management
  • Single-visit RCT with modern, atraumatic instrumentation
  • Intracanal disinfection and careful irrigation
  • Immediate provisional then definitive cap placement
  • Post-op antibiotics and follow-up monitoring
